introduction

Most of the diffuse infrastructure of companies like Amazon remains hidden for the everyday consumer â€“ and even for the critical observer. Apart from huge Amazon warehouses located in more rural areas, which can sometimes be perceived when travelling along motorways, the processes behind those physical instances remain veiled. Occasionally, by looking closely, you can also recognise the mostly anonymous parcel delivery services that move in white, unlabelled vans in urban areas. Most of Amazonâ€™s services are outsourced to third party companies, making it hard to track down the course of action and operation of the multinational company. Return is a research approach that tries to hunt down the infrastructure of returned parcels. The aim of the study is to disclose the Amazon return infrastructure and to consider it in relation to precarious labour. The data collected so far is mostly from Germany. The next step is to extend this network to other European countries in order to draw further conclusions from the processes. This is where you come into play.

method

The research method uses GPS Trackers that need to be ordered at Amazon and that have to be returned straight after they arrive. There are a few tracker types that work and that can be ordered without shipping costs from the following countries: Germany, France, Italy, Netherlands, Spain, Sweden, Turky. These are the countries that officially have Amazon domains which makes the ordering very easy.

If you're located in a country that is not included in the list it is still possible to order from a foreign Amazon domain. e.g. Austria, Belgium, Czech Republic, Denmark, Finland, Luxemburg, Russia, Serbia and Switerland should be able to place orders without shipping costs via the amazon.de domain.

Please indiviually check on which domain you're able to buy one of the trackers without shipping costs. Choose one (preferably the one with the highest battery power!) and order it.
